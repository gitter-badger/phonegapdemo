About

This app was created to make the lives of Bermuda's Recreational Lobster Divers and Spearfishers easier for reporting their catches to the Department of Environmental Protection as well as to track for yourself. The app contains the rules for lobster diving, spearfishing, an island map, emergency contact info, and other useful information for lobster diving and spearfishing!

Please submit any feedback on this app so that we may improve the experience for you!

Lobster Diving Rules

You are permitted to:

  1. Take lobsters during the spiny lobster season from September 1st to March 31st each year
  2. Take, with the use of an approved noose, a maximum of two lobsters in any twenty-four (24) hour period. Spears, harpoons, staffs and grains or any other similar devices CANNOT be used to take lobsters.

You are NOT permitted to:

  1. Take, injure, sell, purchase or be in possession of any lobster whether alive or dead at any time when the lobster is bearing eggs.
  2. Use any appliance, apparatus or mechanism that would enable you to breathe when fully submerged in water.
  3. Sell, trade or barter spiny lobsters.
  4. Wring, dismember or separate the tails at sea.
  5. Take any lobsters in any harbour, bay, inlet, creek or sound OR in the area south of the South Channel on the north shore from Commissioner's Point following the South Channel in an easterly direction to buoy 16A (The Narrows). The eastern boundary of the closed area is an imaginary line drawn from Buoy 16A to the western end of Tabacco Bay on the north shore. The area south of an imaginary line between King's Point and Grey's Bridge is also closed to lobster diving.

You are required to:

  1. Carry on your person, or on the vessel, or shore from which you are diving, your licence to take lobsters.
  2. Carry a measuring instrument capable of measuring a spiny lobster for legal size conformity.
  3. Keep a log of your diving activities with respect to catch and area and submit to the Department of Environmental Protection. Using this app is the ideal and preferred method.

Spearfishing Rules

It is an offense:

  • to use a speargun in taking fish
  • to use an aqualung in taking fish by any means
  • to take or injure any lobster by spearfishing
  • to take or injure by spearfishing in any one day more than two fish of any species (other than black rockfish and monkey rockfish (One per day) and lionfish (No limit)
  • to take by spearfishing any fish within one nautical mile of the shore of any of the islands of Bermuda
  • to sell any fish taken by spearfishing

Additional restrictions:

  • Any fish found in a vessel in which an aqualung is being carried or any fish found in the possession of any person equipped with an aqualung shall be presumed to have been taken in contravention of the Fisheries Regulations until the contrary is proved
  • Any person found swimming within an area in which spearfishing is prohibited by the Fisheries Regulations, while carrying a spear, harpoon or other similar device, or swimming in an area in which the taking of lobsters by diving is prohibited, while carrying an instrument capable of taking a lobster other than a spear, harpoon or other similar device commits an offense.
  • Subject to the Fisheries Regulations, any person found with an instrument capable of taking a lobster, or with a spear, harpoon or other similar device and an aqualugn in his possession, either when in a vessel or on his person, while swimming, commits an offense.

Recommendation

  • Spearfishers targeting rockfish should use a spear with a slip tip to increase the likelihood of retaining the fish.
